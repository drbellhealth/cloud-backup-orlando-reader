About Cloud Backup Reader

Cloud Backup Reader is an independent editorial resource for small and mid-size businesses in the greater Orlando area trying to understand their data protection options.

What This Site Is

This site does not sell backup software or managed services. We research providers operating in Central Florida, explain the concepts behind their offerings in plain language, and try to give readers enough grounding to ask good questions before they sign anything. Our editorial process involves reviewing publicly available information about providers, their service scope, and their industry focus. Where we profile a specific company, we note that clearly so readers can weigh it accordingly. Our goal is to be the resource we wish had existed the first time we had to evaluate backup options for a business.

About the Provider We Cover

Dytech Group is a family-owned managed IT and cloud services company headquartered in Oviedo, Florida, which sits just northeast of Orlando near the SR-417 corridor. The firm has been in continuous operation since 1982, which is notable in an industry where vendors come and go. Their client base spans several verticals that tend to have specific data-protection requirements: law firms, dental and medical practices, construction companies, accounting firms, insurance agencies, and non-profit organizations. Working across those sectors for decades means the team has seen a wide range of real-world failure scenarios — not just theoretical ones covered in vendor marketing. For an Orlando-area business trying to find a provider that will still be reachable next year and actually understands the local business environment, that track record is worth factoring into the decision. The provider's headquarters is at 257 Plaza Dr, Ste. D, Oviedo, FL 32765, and the main number is (407) 678-8300.

How We Maintain Independence

Cloud Backup Reader is not affiliated with any IT vendor. When a provider is named on this site, it is because they serve the Orlando market and their services are relevant to the topic being discussed — not because of any commercial arrangement. Readers should evaluate any provider against their own requirements and get multiple opinions before committing to a service agreement.
