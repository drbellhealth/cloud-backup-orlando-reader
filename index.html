Cloud Backup for Orlando Businesses: Start Here

If you have ever wondered whether your business data is actually safe — or just assumed it is — this guide is for you.

Most small-business owners in Orlando set up some form of backup years ago and have not thought much about it since. That is understandable; there are only so many hours in the day. But backup technology has changed considerably, and the threats have changed even faster. Ransomware — malicious software that locks or destroys your files and demands payment — now routinely targets backup copies specifically, because attackers know that is what you would use to recover. On top of that, many businesses discover too late that their Microsoft 365 email and documents are not automatically protected the way they assumed. Microsoft keeps its servers running, but it does not guarantee that your individual files are recoverable after accidental deletion or a security incident. This guide walks through what a modern backup strategy looks like, what terms like RTO and RPO actually mean for a business your size, and what to look for when you talk to a provider in the Orlando area.
