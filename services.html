Cloud Backup & Disaster Recovery Services in Orlando

Below is a plain-English summary of the main services a business like yours would typically consider when building a reliable backup and recovery plan.

Core Backup & Continuity Services

  • Automated daily (or more frequent) cloud backup of servers, workstations, and NAS devices
  • Microsoft 365 backup — email, SharePoint, OneDrive, and Teams data your tenant does not protect on its own
  • Image-level backup: a full snapshot of a server so it can be restored to new hardware, not just individual files
  • Offsite replication to a geographically separate data center, so a local disaster does not take out both copies
  • Immutable backup copies — storage that cannot be altered or deleted, even by ransomware with admin credentials
  • Air-gapped copies that are physically or logically isolated from your live network
  • Disaster Recovery as a Service (DRaaS) — the ability to spin up your servers in the cloud if physical hardware is gone
  • Backup as a Service (BaaS) — managed, monitored backup so someone checks that it ran correctly every day
  • Defined RTO and RPO targets — agreeing in advance on how fast you need to be back up (RTO) and how much data loss is acceptable (RPO)
  • Tested restore schedules — periodic drills proving your backup actually works
  • Compliance-aligned retention policies for industries with specific record-keeping rules

Managed Backup & Disaster-Recovery-as-a-Service

Managed Backup & Disaster-Recovery-as-a-Service The core difference between managed backup and doing it yourself is that someone is watching the alerts every day — not just you, when you happen to remember to check. Backup as a Service (BaaS) means a provider configures, monitors, and maintains your backup jobs, confirms successful completion, and flags failures before they turn into a crisis. Disaster Recovery as a Service (DRaaS) goes a step further: if your physical office becomes inaccessible — because of fire, flooding, or a storm like Hurricane Ian in 2022 — your workloads can be started in the cloud within a defined time window. That time window is your RTO, short for Recovery Time Objective: the maximum amount of time you can afford to be offline. A managed engagement locks in a specific RTO so you are not guessing under pressure.

Cloud, Microsoft 365 & SaaS Backup

Cloud, Microsoft 365 & SaaS Backup Many businesses assume that storing files in Microsoft 365 — OneDrive, SharePoint, Exchange Online — means those files are backed up. That is not quite accurate. Microsoft's shared-responsibility model means they keep their infrastructure available, but protecting your specific data from accidental deletion, ransomware, or a departing employee's actions is your responsibility. The same principle applies to other cloud applications: QuickBooks Online, Salesforce, Google Workspace. Each platform publishes its own shared-responsibility policy, and most shift more responsibility to the customer than users expect. A dedicated Microsoft 365 backup solution pulls copies of your mailboxes, Teams messages, SharePoint sites, and OneDrive files into a separate system — one that you control and can restore from independently of Microsoft's own support process.

Ransomware-Resilient, Immutable & Air-Gapped Backups

Ransomware-Resilient, Immutable & Air-Gapped Backups Ransomware operators have adapted. Early variants encrypted your working files and waited for you to pay. Modern attacks target connected backup repositories first, because attackers know that eliminating your recovery option forces the payment conversation. The technical response to this is immutable storage — backup copies written in a way that cannot be modified or deleted for a defined period, even by an account with administrator credentials. A separate layer of protection is the air-gapped copy: a set of backup data with no live network path from your environment to the storage location, so malware that spreads across your network cannot reach it. Neither of these is expensive in the context of what a ransomware incident actually costs, but both require intentional configuration. They do not happen automatically with a basic cloud sync.

Server, NAS & Endpoint Backup with Replication

Server, NAS & Endpoint Backup with Replication For businesses running a physical or virtual server — whether it hosts your accounting software, your practice management system, or your file shares — image-level backup is usually the right approach. Rather than backing up individual files, an image-level backup captures the entire state of the server: the operating system, applications, configurations, and data together. If the hardware fails, you restore the image to new hardware and pick up close to where you left off. The RPO (Recovery Point Objective — how far back in time you would have to roll) depends on how frequently images are captured; some setups run every fifteen minutes. Offsite replication copies those images to a geographically separate location continuously, so a single-site event does not leave you with nothing. NAS devices and individual workstations can also be included in the same managed policy.

What Onboarding a Backup Engagement Looks Like

What Onboarding a Backup Engagement Looks Like A new backup engagement with a competent provider typically starts with a discovery conversation: what data do you have, where does it live, what applications are business-critical, and what would an hour of downtime actually cost you? From there, the provider maps out an RTO and RPO for each system — not a single number for the whole business, because your billing server and your marketing files probably have different recovery priorities. Configuration follows: agents installed on servers and workstations, cloud connectors set up for SaaS applications, retention schedules set to match any compliance requirements. The engagement is not complete until a restore has been tested. A backup that has never been restored is a theory, not a guarantee. Any provider worth working with will schedule test restores at regular intervals and document the results.
